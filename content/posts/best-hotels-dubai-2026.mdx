## The Scout's Take

Every Dubai hotel wants to be called the "best". What we care about is: does the hotel work for the way you actually travel?

If you're the kind of person who values morning rituals — a quiet swim, a proper gym, a breakfast worth queueing for at home — **The Lana (Dorchester Collection)** should be your first call. Opened on the Marasi Bay Marina in 2024, it has the most thoughtful wellness programme of any new Dubai opening: a 25-metre lap pool (rare for a city hotel), a Dior Spa, and a Dior Fitness studio where your session is programmed by a qualified coach, not a bored receptionist with a stopwatch.

Compare that to **Burj Al Arab Jumeirah** — still iconic, still the "seven-star" sail — but its morning routine is built for spectacle, not substance. The pool deck is stunning and breakfast is theatrical, but there's no serious fitness space, and the spa follows the classic luxury-hotel playbook of heavy oil massages rather than recovery-focused wellness.

For mornings, The Lana wins. For "I'm here to be seen", Burj Al Arab is still unbeaten.

## Our 10 for 2026

**1. The Lana, Dorchester Collection** — Downtown / Marasi Bay. Best in class for wellness travellers. Dior Spa, Dior Fitness, the only serious lap pool downtown.

**2. Atlantis The Royal** — Palm Jumeirah. Celebrity openings have made it the most photographed hotel in the city. Cloud 22 infinity pool, 17 restaurants, private beach access.

**3. Burj Al Arab Jumeirah** — Umm Suqeim Road. The icon. Book for the view from Skyview Bar, stay for the Rolls-Royce airport transfer.

**4. Armani Hotel Dubai** — Inside Burj Khalifa. Minimalist luxury by Giorgio Armani himself. For guests who don't need extra — just extra-quiet.

**5. Bulgari Resort Dubai** — Jumeirah Bay Island. A private island connected to the mainland by a 300-metre bridge. The closest thing to Italian coastal living in the Gulf.

**6. One&Only The Palm** — West Crescent, Palm Jumeirah. Boutique feel, private beach, and a Michelin-starred restaurant (STAY by Yannick Alléno).

**7. Four Seasons Resort Dubai at Jumeirah Beach** — A classic for Four Seasons loyalists. Beach access, one of Dubai's best kids' programmes, and the strongest club lounge in the city.

**8. Address Sky View** — Downtown. Connected to the Dubai Mall via sky-bridges. The best-located hotel for serious shoppers.

**9. Jumeirah Al Qasr** — Part of Madinat Jumeirah. A resort-within-a-resort with Venetian-style waterways and direct access to Souk Madinat.

**10. Mandarin Oriental Jumeirah** — Jumeirah Beach. The most service-forward hotel in Dubai. Every request is answered before you've finished asking it.

## Neighborhood Intelligence: High-End Fitness Near Downtown

Hotel gyms in Dubai have improved — The Lana's Dior Fitness is proof — but if you're serious about training, the real scene sits outside the hotels.

For the Downtown Dubai and Marasi Bay area, here's where serious gym-goers actually train:

- **Warehouse Gym, Al Quoz** — a 10-minute taxi from Downtown. Olympic lifting platforms, a full CrossFit rig, and strongman equipment. Day passes available for hotel guests.
- **Fit Republik, Dubai Sports City** — further out, but worth the trip. Boxing, reformer pilates, and recovery rooms with cryotherapy and red-light beds.
- **Symmetry Gym, DIFC** — the closest to The Lana. Personal-training focus, small-group classes, and the cleanest functional-fitness space in the city.
