Physical SIM cards are dead. That's not hyperbole — iPhones sold in the US have shipped without a SIM tray since 2022, and Apple's 2026 lineup makes that true globally. Samsung's Galaxy S25 Ultra is eSIM-only in 12 more countries this year. If you're travelling in 2026, you need an eSIM.

This guide walks through what an eSIM actually is, why it's now the only sensible way to stay connected abroad, and exactly which provider to pick for your trip.

## What Is an eSIM?

An eSIM ("embedded SIM") is a tiny chip soldered inside your phone that can hold multiple carrier profiles at once. Instead of a plastic card, your carrier sends you a QR code — you scan it, the profile installs, and you're on their network in under 90 seconds.

In practical travel terms: you can land at Bangkok airport, open your phone, and already be connected to a Thai network at local prices. No hunting for a kiosk, no queuing, no swapping your home SIM into a tiny plastic envelope you'll definitely lose.

## Why Physical SIMs Are Dead in 2026

Three things killed them:

1. **Flagship phones dropped the tray.** Every 2026 iPhone, Pixel, and most flagship Samsungs either have no SIM tray or keep only a "backup" tray that the manufacturer actively discourages using.
2. **5G speeds on eSIMs now match local SIMs.** In 2023 there was a real speed difference. In 2026, eSIM carriers use the same 5G towers as local networks — the speed cap was always marketing, not physics.
3. **Coverage caught up.** Airalo alone covers 200+ destinations. Yesim covers 180. Between them there's almost nowhere an eSIM traveller can't operate.

## How Much Data Do You Actually Need?

Most travellers massively over-buy data. Here's a realistic calibration from our testing:

- **1GB per week** — if you're mostly on hotel WiFi and only want maps, messages, and occasional browsing on the go.
- **3GB per week** — if you're actively using Google Maps, social media, and streaming music outside WiFi.
- **5GB+ per week** — if you're video-calling, working remotely, or streaming Netflix on mobile.
- **Unlimited** — digital nomads, content creators, anyone who treats their phone as their primary internet.

For most two-week trips, **3GB is plenty**. Don't let the sales funnel push you into a 20GB "pro" plan you'll never use.

## Privacy Shield: The Part No eSIM Company Will Tell You

Here's the part no eSIM company will tell you. When you roam on your home carrier, your home carrier logs everywhere you go. Every cell-tower handshake is recorded and retained per UK data laws for 12 months. It's legal, routine, and increasingly used for everything from insurance risk profiling to government queries.

Using a travel eSIM puts a privacy layer between your home identity and your travel activity. The local carrier sees an anonymous eSIM profile, not your home phone number. For most people that's a nice-to-have. For journalists, lawyers, and executives, it's essential.
