There is a major misunderstanding in the travel world that causes constant confusion for holidaymakers. Many travellers believe that any flight out of the UK automatically comes with ATOL protection.

Let's clear this up immediately: **if you are booking a standalone flight or hotel directly with a provider, it has absolutely nothing to do with ATOL.**

## 🔍 The Core Concept: What is ATOL Actually For?

ATOL stands for Air Travel Organisers' Licensing. Managed by the UK Civil Aviation Authority (CAA), it is a financial safety net designed specifically for package holidays and trips booked through third-party travel agents.

**The safety net:** if a licensed UK travel company goes bust, ATOL ensures you get a full refund or, if you are already abroad, pays to bring you home safely.

**The catch:** ATOL only applies when you buy a holiday bundle (for example, flight + hotel, or flight + car hire) from a single travel firm in a single transaction.

## ❌ The Direct Booking Exception: Why ATOL Disappears

When you cut out the middleman and build a "Do-It-Yourself" holiday, the legal framework completely changes.

### 1. Booking a flight directly with an airline (the e-ticket rule)

If you use [JetMeAway Flights](/flights) to compare routes and then go straight to an airline like British Airways, easyJet, or Ryanair to buy a seat, you are not ATOL protected. Under UK law, scheduled airlines selling their own seats are entirely exempt from the ATOL scheme.

Instead, you are issued a valid direct e-ticket immediately upon payment. This e-ticket acts as a legally binding, direct contract between you and the airline. Because of this direct contract, the airline company itself is directly responsible for your refund if anything goes wrong. If they cancel your flight, delay you, or change your schedule, they are legally obligated under UK261 passenger rights laws to refund you, rebook you, or pay you compensation. You do not need ATOL because the airline holds the direct legal and financial liability to put things right.

### 2. Booking a standalone hotel room

If you use [JetMeAway Hotels](/hotels) to secure your room, ATOL is non-existent. Accommodation-only transactions never include ATOL certificates because there is no air travel component involved in the booking.

## 🛡️ How to Stay Protected When You Book Standalone Travel

Comparing and booking standalone options often secures you the best room rates, direct airline loyalty points, and flexible seat selections. Since you do not have ATOL backing you up on standalone bookings, you should put alternative protections in place:

**Pay via credit card.** Under Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act, your credit card provider protects purchases between £100 and £30,000 if a travel provider goes completely bankrupt.

**Get SAFI insurance.** When buying [travel insurance](/insurance) through JetMeAway, make sure the policy includes Scheduled Airline Failure Insurance (SAFI) or End Supplier Failure cover to protect you against independent airline insolvency.

**Check cancellation terms.** Keep track of the cancellation windows for your standalone hotels, so you can cancel or change dates if your flight gets disrupted.
