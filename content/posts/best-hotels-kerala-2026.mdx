## The Scout's Take: Backwater Stillness or Cliffside Ocean?

Every Kerala five-star pitches a different version of slowness. The question is whether you want *backwater stillness* or *cliffside ocean drama*.

If you're the kind of traveller who comes for the iconic Kerala photograph — the wooden houseboat drifting through coconut palms, the morning mist on Vembanad Lake, the cook on board grilling karimeen for lunch — **Kumarakom Lake Resort** is the answer. It's the heritage backwater stay in pure form: 28 acres of lakefront, traditional Kerala-style *tharavadu* villas with private pools, a meandering swimming pool that's actually built around the resort, and the original wooden architecture that defined the high-end backwater hotel category in the early 2000s. Mornings here are still — genuinely still, not Instagram-still — and the resort's own kettuvallam houseboat fleet drifts in and out at dawn.

Compare that to **The Leela Kovalam** — same Kerala, completely different mood. Built on a cliff above two private crescent-bay beaches at the southern tip of Kerala, it's the dramatic-coastline counterpart to the calm-backwater play. The hotel sits 50 metres above sea level, with a glass-walled lobby that opens straight onto the cliff edge and an infinity pool that genuinely seems to fall into the Arabian Sea. Sunset cocktails on the terrace are the southern-India benchmark experience. The two private beaches are accessible only by hotel pathway. It's the romantic-getaway Kerala, the post-honeymoon-decompression Kerala, the I-want-to-see-the-sea-from-my-bed Kerala.

For the iconic Kerala photograph and the slowest mornings of your life, Kumarakom Lake Resort wins. For dramatic coastal luxury and sunset cocktails, The Leela Kovalam is the smarter call.

## Beyond the Hotel: 10 Things to Do in Kerala (2026)

Kerala rewards travellers who slow down and don't try to compress the state into 5 days. Here's the prioritised list:

**1. Alleppey Backwater Houseboat Overnight** — Non-negotiable. Book a private 1-3 bedroom *kettuvallam* through your main hotel or a vetted CGH-Earth-style operator. Lunchtime departure, evening anchorage, fresh-caught fish dinner, dawn awakening on the water. The single most photographed Kerala experience.

**2. Munnar Tea Plantations & Eravikulam National Park** — Tea estates carpeting the hills at 1,500m altitude, plus Eravikulam National Park where the endangered Nilgiri tahr (mountain goat) roams free. Tea Museum at Munnar town gives you the production-line context. Half-day for the plantations, separate morning for Eravikulam.

**3. Periyar Tiger Reserve Boat Safari & Bamboo Rafting** — Periyar's lake safari is the gentlest way to see wild elephants in Kerala. Book the 7am boat for the highest sighting odds. The half-day bamboo-rafting trek is the under-rated alternative — small group, on-foot through the reserve buffer zone with a tribal guide.

**4. Fort Kochi Chinese Fishing Nets at Sunset** — The 14th-century shore-operated fishing nets are a UNESCO-symbolic Kerala image. Walk the Vasco da Gama Square promenade at golden hour, watch the nets being lowered, eat at one of the fish-market shacks afterwards (the fish is grilled in front of you for ₹400-600).

**5. Mattancherry Palace & Paradesi Synagogue** — The 1568 Mattancherry Dutch Palace holds Kerala's best Hindu mural collection. The Paradesi Synagogue (the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth) is 5 minutes' walk away — a remnant of Kerala's 2,000-year Jewish heritage. Allow 2 hours for both.

**6. Kathakali Performance** — Kerala's traditional dance-drama, with elaborate face painting, elaborate eye-language, and stories from the *Mahabharata*. The Kerala Kathakali Centre in Fort Kochi runs nightly performances; arrive 90 minutes before curtain to watch the make-up application — that's half the show.

**7. Kovalam Lighthouse & Hawa Beach** — Kovalam's two main beaches, separated by the iconic red-and-white striped lighthouse. Lighthouse Beach is busier and shack-lined; Hawa Beach (north of the lighthouse) is quieter and better for swimming. Climb the lighthouse for the best view of both.

**8. Varkala Cliff Sunset** — 45 minutes north of Kovalam, Varkala's red-sandstone cliffs drop dramatically into the Arabian Sea. The cliff-top walking path is lined with cafes, yoga studios, and surf-shop boutiques. Time your visit for late afternoon and stay for sunset over the cliff edge.

**9. Authentic Ayurveda Treatment** — Be specific: a "tourist Ayurvedic massage" at a beach hotel is a tourist spa, not Ayurveda. Real Ayurveda is doctor-prescribed, multi-day, and follows a *prakriti* (body-type) consultation. Book through CGH Earth, Niraamaya, or (for the serious option) Kalari Kovilakom. Minimum useful stay is 7 nights; 14-21 for a panchakarma cycle.

**10. Theyyam Ritual Performance** — North Kerala only, October to May. Theyyam is a 1,500-year-old ritual where village performers possessed by deities dance in elaborate red-and-gold costumes. Performances are religious, not theatrical, and start at 11pm and run until dawn. Book through Taj Bekal or a Kannur-based homestay — independent ritual schedules are unreliable.
