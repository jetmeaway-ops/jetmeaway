The era of "clear your cookies" is over. Airline AI is smarter than that. To find cheap flights from the UK in 2026, you need to think like a Scout — and you need to understand that **the flight isn't where the big money is**. The hotel is.

### 1. The London Multi-Airport Play

If you live in the South East, don't just search "Heathrow." Use the **LON** code to include Gatwick, Stansted, and Luton.

**The Scout's Tip:** For European hops, **London City (LCY)** is often overlooked. While the base fare might be £20 more, you save 2 hours in security and £30 in transport costs.

### 2. The "3-Month Sweep"

In 2026, the sweet spot for booking European short-haul is precisely **72 to 90 days** out. For long-haul (USA/Asia), aim for **5 months**.

**Scout Intelligence:** Use our "Super Fast" search engine to check Tuesday and Wednesday departures. Most UK travellers fly Friday-to-Monday; moving your trip by 24 hours can save you up to 40% on a BA or Virgin fare.

Airlines track your IP and location to adjust prices.

## The Real Strategy: Hotel First

Here's the move most travellers get wrong. They hunt the flight for weeks, squeeze every last pound out of the airfare, then book a hotel at the last minute — when rates are at their highest.

**Flip it.** Lock in the flight at the best price you find in your 72–90 day window, then **immediately** book the hotel through JetMeAway while **non-refundable rates are still available**.

Why this saves you more:

- **Non-refundable hotel rates are 20–40% cheaper than flexible rates.** If your flight is booked and you know you're going, there's no reason to pay for flexibility you won't use.
- **Hotel inventory tightens faster than flight inventory.** Airlines can add seats with bigger aircraft; hotels can't add rooms. The earlier you lock in, the better your suite.
- **The exchange rate risk is on the hotel, not you.** Pay in GBP today and the rate is frozen. Wait three months and you're exposed to currency swings.

For most European trips, booking your hotel within 24 hours of booking your flight will save you more money than any "airline hack" you read online.
