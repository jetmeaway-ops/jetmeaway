Egypt isn't one holiday. It's five different ones sharing the same passport stamp. The country you fly into depends entirely on what you want — ancient history, desert silence, underwater coral, Mediterranean beach, or the slow motion of a Nile cruise.

Most UK travellers default to Sharm El Sheikh or Hurghada, book a package, and never leave the hotel complex. That's fine — but it's one-fifth of what Egypt actually offers. Here's the Scout's breakdown of all five.

### 1. Cairo & Giza: The Ancient Capital

**The Draw:** The Pyramids of Giza, the Sphinx, and as of November 2025 — the fully opened Grand Egyptian Museum, the largest archaeological museum in the world.

**Stay:** A rooftop room at the **Marriott Mena House** with direct pyramid views, or **The Nile Ritz-Carlton** for a Tahrir Square base close to the Egyptian Museum.

**How long:** 3 nights minimum. The Grand Egyptian Museum alone takes a full day, and Saqqara (the step pyramid) is often missed but is arguably more impressive than Giza up close.

**Scout Tip:** Book the sunrise pyramid visit slot (6am entry). By 9am the tour coaches arrive and the magic evaporates.

### 2. Nile Cruise: Luxor to Aswan

**The Draw:** A four-night sail down the Nile, with temple stops at Karnak, Luxor, Edfu, Kom Ombo, and Philae. You sleep on the boat; the scenery rearranges itself overnight.

**Vibe:** Slow, historical, social. Dinners are communal. You will make friends with retired Germans and 30-year-old Australian backpackers in the same week.

**Best for:** First-time visitors who want the "greatest hits" version of ancient Egypt without arranging their own logistics.

**Top Scout Picks:**
- **Oberoi Zahra** — the most refined cruise on the river, 27 cabins only, oil-painting interiors.
- **Sanctuary Sun Boat IV** — a balance of luxury and scale, with the best guide team on the Nile.
- **Mövenpick MS Royal Lily** — mid-tier price point, excellent food, popular with UK guests.

**2026 Update:** Most cruises now include the fully restored Avenue of Sphinxes walk at Luxor — a 2.7km ceremonial path reopened in 2021 and finally fully lit at night.

### 3. Red Sea: Hurghada, Marsa Alam & El Gouna

**The Draw:** All-inclusive beach resorts, 28°C sea temperatures year-round, and some of the best diving in the world.

**El Gouna** is the upmarket option — a purpose-built resort town with no touts, marina restaurants, and a different vibe to the package-heavy Hurghada strip.

**Marsa Alam** is quieter and further south, with direct access to Dolphin House reef and the genuinely remote Fury Shoals.

**Vibe:** Beach-and-pool with excellent diving on the doorstep. Flight time from the UK is around 5.5 hours, making it one of the warmest short-haul destinations you can reach without a long flight.

**Top Scout Picks:**
- **Steigenberger Aldau Beach, Hurghada** — reliable 5-star, excellent reef access from the beach.
- **The Chedi El Gouna** — boutique luxury, private beach, the most stylish room on the Red Sea.
- **Marsa Shagra Village, Marsa Alam** — eco-diving resort, no TV, ideal for PADI-certified divers.

### 4. Sharm El Sheikh: The All-Inclusive Beach Holiday

**The Draw:** Reliable sun, short flights, Sinai Peninsula backdrop, and some of the most aggressive all-inclusive pricing anywhere in the Mediterranean.

**Vibe:** Family-friendly, package-holiday territory. Naama Bay for nightlife, Nabq for quiet, Ras Um Sid for the best reef.

**Best for:** Families, first-time Egypt visitors, and anyone who wants zero holiday logistics — just pay once, fly, collapse onto a sun lounger for seven days.

**Top Scout Picks:**
- **Four Seasons Resort Sharm El Sheikh** — the premium option, terraced gardens, private reef.
- **Rixos Premium Seagate** — all-inclusive done well, 13 pools, 18 restaurants.
- **Baron Resort Sharm El Sheikh** — mid-tier, consistently 4.5+ star reviews, excellent kids' clubs.

### 5. Alexandria & The North Coast

**The Draw:** Mediterranean Egypt — cooler, whiter, and dramatically different to the Red Sea. Alexandria has the Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Roman catacombs, and the best seafood in the country.

**Vibe:** Low-key, cultured, and largely un-touristed by Western travellers. This is where wealthy Cairenes go for the summer.

**Best for:** Second-time Egypt visitors who've done the pyramids and want something quieter.

**Scout Tip:** Stay at the **Four Seasons Alexandria San Stefano** for the beach, or the **Paradise Inn Le Metropole** in the old quarter for the heritage experience.

### Cost Comparison (Mid-Range, 2026)

- **Cairo 3-star + guide:** ~£80/night including pyramid excursions.
- **Nile cruise 5-star (4 nights):** ~£900 per person, all-inclusive with excursions.
- **Red Sea all-inclusive 5-star:** ~£110/night including food and drinks.
- **Sharm El Sheikh 7-night package from UK:** ~£550 per person flights + hotel.
- **Alexandria 4-star:** ~£70/night.

Egypt is one of the strongest value destinations in the world for 2026 — the Egyptian pound has weakened significantly against sterling, which means your £100 goes further than in almost any Mediterranean alternative.

> **Scout Verdict:** Combine two. Fly into Cairo for 3 nights, take an internal flight to Luxor for a 4-night Nile cruise, then finish with 3 nights in Hurghada. That's the canonical 10-day Egypt trip — and you'll see more of the country than 80% of package tourists manage in a week.

## Practical 2026 Notes

- **Visa:** UK passport holders need an e-visa (£20-25) issued online before departure. Takes 3-5 working days.
- **Currency:** Egyptian Pound (EGP). Cards accepted in hotels; cash preferred in markets and for tipping.
- **Tipping (baksheesh):** Expected everywhere. Budget £20-30 per person per week in small notes.
- **Best months:** October to April. Summer in Upper Egypt hits 45°C; avoid June-August for Luxor and Aswan.
- **Ramadan 2027:** February 7 - March 8. If you're planning ahead, restaurants outside resort complexes may have reduced daytime hours during this period.
